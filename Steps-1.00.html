Problem Statement 1

        According to the Agricultural Marketing Resource Center, “Thousands of California growers continue to harvest avocadoes on around 60,000 acres a year.” These statistics make California avocados appear easy to grow and harvest, but they're not. Ultimately, a successful crop is dependent on crucial irrigation water. In general, it takes 74 gallons of water to produce one pound of avocados and drought-stricken California has 95% of avocados. In 2015, California experienced its warmest February in 120 years, 8 degrees above the 20th-century average and the recorded sales lost was $1.8 billion.
